## The Self-Hosting Backlash

But the tide is turning. We're seeing a growing movement toward self-hosting and open-source alternatives, driven by three key factors:

1. **Cost transparency** - Companies are finally realizing that paying $2M/year in cloud fees doesn't actually scale when your usage grows
2. **Vendor lock-in** - The "AI integration" trap where you build your entire workflow around one vendor's ecosystem only to discover you're paying 5x the market rate
3. **Open-source maturity** - Tools like Cirrus Labs joining OpenAI, and emerging open-source alternatives that deliver comparable functionality without the enterprise tax

## What You Should Do Today

1. **Audit your AI spending** - Look at every SaaS tool that tacked on "AI features" and ask if you're actually using them or just paying for buzzword compliance
2. **Evaluate self-hosting** - For non-critical workloads, the cost of running models on your own infrastructure is becoming increasingly attractive
3. **Demand transparency** - Ask vendors to provide clear pricing breakdowns for AI features vs. core functionality
4. **Build in flexibility** - Design systems that can swap between different AI providers without massive rewrites

The future isn't about choosing between AWS, Google, and Microsoft's AI offerings. It's about building software that works on your terms, not theirs.
