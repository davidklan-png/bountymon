AI coding agents aren't just the future—they're actively rewriting the rules of software development and SaaS economics. In a stunning move, Chamath Palihapitiya's latest AI coding startup just secured $135M in Series A funding, with Palihapitiya taking the CEO role himself. This isn't just another AI tool; it's a statement that coding assistance has become mission-critical infrastructure.

But the real story isn't the funding—it's the economics. As one developer told TechCrunch about using AI agents for calendar and email planning, "That person was spending at a run rate of $30,000 a year for this." That's subscription fatigue in its purest form. Enterprises are bleeding cash on tools that promise productivity but deliver incremental gains at exponential cost.

Meanwhile, the build vs buy calculus is shifting dramatically. Rippling's latest move to become "your entire data stack" shows traditional SaaS players sensing the threat. They're consolidating not just to capture more revenue, but to prevent the fragmentation that makes self-hosted alternatives increasingly attractive.

The most telling signal? Amazon's $13B AI infrastructure investment in India. When cloud giants bet big on AI infrastructure, they're not just betting on the technology—they're betting on the underlying premise that computing cycles should be owned, not rented.

For software buyers, the implications are clear:

1. **Expect SaaS pricing wars** as incumbents battle AI disruptors
2. **Self-hosting is no longer fringe**—it's becoming the sovereign choice
3. **AI agents will fragment** before consolidating, creating chaos and opportunity
4. **Developer productivity tools** are becoming the new battleground for enterprise software

Bountymon exists for this moment. When software costs spiral and enterprise solutions become bloated, the natural alternative emerges: build what you need, control your destiny, and keep the savings. AI coding agents accelerate this shift by making self-hosting dramatically more practical.

The great unbundling has begun. Are you ready to own your stack?
