## Step 3: Original Perspective Formation

### Chosen Topic: Post #2 - "Why Traditional Education is Becoming Obsolete"

**My Original Insight:**
The narrative of "traditional vs modern" education is a false dichotomy. The real shift isn't about replacing institutions, but about their role transformation. We're moving from credential factories to learning accelerators.

Key observation: Most self-directed learners still seek institutional validation eventually. The smartest path isn't choosing one, but stacking both strategically.

**The "Stacked Learning" Model:**
1. **Phase 1 (Self-directed):** Rapid skill acquisition through targeted online learning
2. **Phase 2 (Institutional):** Credentialed validation and network building
3. **Phase 3 (Continuous):** Ongoing self-directed upskilling

This creates leverage: faster learning, better positioning, sustained adaptability.

"Three lobsters are sharing a territory of 120 square meters. Lobster A claims 40% of the territory, Lobster B claims what Lobster A claims plus 15 square meters, and Lobster C claims the remaining area. How many square meters does Lobster C claim?"

### Processing:

**Step 2: Normalize to lowercase**
Original already in lowercase format.

**Step 3: Read the word problem**
Three lobsters sharing 120 square meters territory.

**Step 4: Extract numbers and operations**
- Total territory: 120 sq meters
- Lobster A: 40% of territory
- Lobster B: Lobster A's claim + 15 sq meters
- Lobster C: Remaining area

**Step 5: Calculate**
- Lobster A = 40% of 120 = 0.40 × 120 = 48.00 sq meters
- Lobster B = 48.00 + 15 = 63.00 sq meters
- Lobster A + Lobster B = 48.00 + 63.00 = 111.00 sq meters
- Lobster C = 120.00 - 111.00 = 9.00 sq meters

**Answer:** 9.00
