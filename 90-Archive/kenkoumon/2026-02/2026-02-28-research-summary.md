# Medical Session Intelligence Platform — Summary

## One-Liner

Doctor-patient voice recording + AI analysis for personalized care delivery through psychological profiling and communication adaptation.

## Problem

- 40-80% of medical information is forgotten immediately after visits
- Doctors have 10-15 minutes per patient, can't adapt communication style
- Non-native speakers struggle with medical terminology
- No persistent record of how patients communicate, only what was diagnosed

## Solution

Record medical sessions → AI extracts:
1. **Medical content**: symptoms, diagnosis, treatment plan
2. **Psychological profile**: anxiety level, health literacy, communication style
3. **Actionable outputs**: patient summary, doctor briefing, follow-up plan

## Wedge

Patient brings recording device (phone) → owns their data → shares profile with any doctor. Zero workflow change for doctors initially.

## Market

- Japan: ~160,000 private clinics, ~15,000 mid-size clinics
- TAM: ¥60B-120B annually (clinic subscriptions)
- SOM (3 years): 500-1,000 clinics = ¥300M-600M ARR

## Competitive Gap

Existing clinical AI (Nuance DAX, Abridge) focuses on documentation efficiency. No one is doing **psychological profiling + communication adaptation** for personalized care.

## Revenue Model

| Model | Pricing |
|-------|---------|
| Per-session | ¥500-2,000/visit (patient pays) |
| Clinic subscription | ¥30K-100K/month |
| Patient subscription | ¥1,000/month (owns profile, portable) |

## MVP Path

1. **Manual pilot**: Record 10-20 sessions with single urologist, manual processing
2. **Validate**: Doctor willingness, patient satisfaction, follow-up adherence
3. **Build**: Mobile app + automated analysis if validated

## Key Risks

- Regulatory (medical device classification)
- Privacy (APPI compliance in Japan)
- Doctor adoption (time pressure)
- AI accuracy (hallucination in medical context)

## Defensibility

- Psychological profiling engine (prompt engineering + framework)
- Regulatory compliance moat
- Network effects (more sessions → better profiles)
- Patient-owned data (portability creates lock-in)
