# Comment Draft

## Target Insight
The core insight is powerful: tracking failures transforms them from emotional events to data points.

## Original Angle: The Psychology of Quantification

The reason your rejection tracker works isn't just about momentum—it's about *cognitive reappraisal*.

When you track rejections, you're doing something subtle but profound: you're converting a subjective emotional experience ("they rejected me") into an objective data point (Entry #201). This creates **psychological distance**.

Research on cognitive reappraisal (Gross, 1998; Ochsner & Gross, 2005) shows that reframing emotional events reduces amygdala activation and increases prefrontal cortex engagement. In plain terms: you move from *feeling* the rejection to *observing* the rejection.

The 200/42 ratio is irrelevant. What matters is Entry #1, Entry #2, Entry #3... each one is a small ritual of emotional detachment. The tracker isn't measuring success—it's training you to stop *feeling* the weight of each "no."

This is why the momentum matters more than the ratio. The momentum means you're still doing the reappraisal work. The ratio? That's just noise.

The counterintuitive truth: you're not tracking rejections. You're building a system that makes it impossible for a single rejection to matter.
