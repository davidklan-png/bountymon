# I Didn't Post About International Women's Day. I wrote an article, here's Why.

## Source

- **Platform**: LinkedIn (Pulse Article)
- **Author**: David Klan (you)
- **URL**: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/i-didnt-post-international-womens-day-wrote-article-heres-david-klan-jprgc/
- **Published**: March 10, 2026

## Article Summary

**Core Premise**: LinkedIn's International Women's Day campaign asked users to "celebrate her with a post or video." This article critiques that framing as performative and engagement-driven rather than genuinely appreciative.

### Key Arguments:

1. **Platform Limitation**: LinkedIn's version of "celebrate her" only works if she's in the network. The women who most shaped the author (mom, sisters, teachers) aren't on LinkedIn.

2. **Performative Nature**: The campaign reframes gratitude as content, turning personal appreciation into engagement metrics and personal brand assets.

3. **Invisible Impact**: The women who influenced the author most did it invisibly — no audience, no metrics, no brand strategy. They shaped a human being, not a LinkedIn profile.

4. **Alternative Action**: Instead of posting, the author:
   - Called his mom (talked for an hour, heard a new story)
   - Texted his sisters
   - Thanked his wife
   - Described it as "clean, undeniable feeling of a real human connection"

5. **Challenge to Readers**: Close LinkedIn for 12 minutes. Meditate on the women who actually shaped you. Then call them, not post about them.

### Memorable Lines:

- "That's not celebration. That's someone else's engagement strategy wearing gratitude as a costume, virtue signaling."

- "The real women who changed my life don't need a post. They need a phone call. A visit."

- "That moment will mean more to her than a thousand tagged posts ever could. And it'll mean more to you too. And don't mention LinkedIn."
