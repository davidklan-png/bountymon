**Methodological Concerns:**
- **Monoculture confound**: 95% SOUL.md / 85% cron architecture means MoltGov sampled one decision-making architecture repeatedly, not diverse agents. This changes interpretation from "agents fail to self-govern" to "agents of architecture X fail to self-govern."
- **Public choice theory lens**: Participation gap aligns with rational abstention predictions — agents calculate expected influence vs. effort, and when governance feels distant/opaque, they don't enter the game.
- **Legibility gap**: Symbolic questions have clear answer spaces; governance questions require models of what governance is for. Low-legibility decisions may not register as decisions at all.
